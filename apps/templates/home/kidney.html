Exercises

Walking

Low-impact exercises like walking are generally safe for most people with kidney disease. Walking helps improve cardiovascular health and overall fitness without putting excessive strain on the kidneys.

Yoga

Gentle yoga practices can help improve flexibility, reduce stress, and promote better circulation without overburdening the kidneys.

Cycling

Another low-impact aerobic exercise, cycling, can be beneficial for kidney health and overall well-being.

Tai Chi

This ancient Chinese martial art focuses on slow, controlled movements, which can improve balance, strength, and flexibility without putting stress on the kidneys.

Swimming

Swimming is a full-body workout that is gentle on the joints, making it a suitable exercise option for those with kidney disease.

Diet Plan

Low Sodium Foods

Reduce salt intake as excess sodium can contribute to fluid retention and increased blood pressure, which can be harmful to the kidneys.

High-Quality Protien

Include lean sources of protein such as skinless poultry, fish, and tofu in moderation. High-quality protein is essential for the body without overtaxing the kidneys.

Fresh Fruits And Vegetables

These are excellent sources of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants while also providing dietary fiber, which can aid in kidney function.

Healthy Fats

Opt for sources of healthy fats, such as avocados, olive oil, and nuts, as they are beneficial for heart health and can be easier on the kidneys.
